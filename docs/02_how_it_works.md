# 2. How does the ExELang Consortium work?

## Structure and Governance

The ExELang Consortium is operated by three custodians: Okko Räsänen (Tampere University), Marvin Lavechin, and Sho Tsuji. They are responsible for using the shared datasets within the scope defined in this document. Corpus collectors retain primary control over their own datasets at all times.

## Prerequisites for Membership

To join the ExELang Consortium, a corpus collector must satisfy two conditions:

1. Deposit their dataset in a recognized scientific archive — HomeBank, Databrary, or the Language Archive (TLA).
2. Grant the custodians access to their dataset through that archive.

Once these two steps are complete, the corpus collector is a member of the consortium.

> **Note for IRBs and legal departments:** The ExELang Consortium does not create a new legal basis for data access. Access rights are entirely governed by the corpus collector's existing agreement with their scientific archive and the archive's access policies. The consortium defines only the permissible scope of use for data already accessible under those archive policies.

### The Language Archive (TLA)

The Language Archive stores a large collection of data from language materials (audio, video and texts). It is part of the Max Planck Institute for Psycholinguistics in Nijmegen, the Netherlands. Access to different collections varies from open to restricted.

To join TLA, a corpus collector must:

1. Deposit their dataset(s) (see instructions)
2. Sign the deposit agreement shared by TLA
3. Inform TLA that you wish to grant access to Sho TSUJI (stsuji@ens.psl.eu), Okko RÄSÄNEN (rasaneno@tuni.fi), Marvin LAVECHIN (marvinlvn).

### Databrary

Databrary stores a large collection of data from behavioral science including video and audio. It is part of New York University in the US. Access to the collection is restricted and requires institutional authorization.

To join Databrary, a corpus collector must:

1. Create a volume to deposit their dataset(s).
2. Give access to Sho TSUJI, Okko RÄSÄNEN, and Marvin LAVECHIN.

### HomeBank

HomeBank stores a large collection of daylong child-centered audio recordings and associated processing tools. It is part of the TalkBank system at Carnegie Mellon University in the US. Access to data is password protected and restricted to members of the HomeBank consortium.

To join HomeBank, a corpus collector must:

1. Contact the HomeBank steering committee to deposit their dataset(s) and join the consortium.

## Scope of Custodian Access

Upon joining, custodians receive access to the corpus collector's dataset. This access is granted for the following defined purpose only:

**Permitted use:**
Use of audio recordings and human annotations for the development, training, evaluation, and benchmarking of automated speech and language processing models (e.g., child vocalization detection, speaker diarization, language identification).

**Not permitted without prior consultation:**
Use of corpus data to answer substantive scientific research questions unrelated to model performance (e.g., cross-linguistic variation in child-directed speech, language acquisition trajectories). Such uses require corpus collector notification and offer of co-authorship or data withdrawal (see Section 5: Publication Rules).

## Benefits of joining the ExELang Consortium

By joining the ExELang Consortium, corpus collectors receive the following benefits:

- The presence of the dataset in model training and benchmarking increases the likelihood that models will perform well on similar data collected by the same corpus collector in the future.
- The dataset will receive formal citation and acknowledgement in all publications using it, providing academic recognition.
- Corpus collectors are notified of any proposed use beyond model development/benchmarking, and retain the right to withdraw their data or join as co-authors.
