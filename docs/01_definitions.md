# 1. Definitions

The following terms are used throughout this document with the meanings defined below.

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Corpus Collector** | A researcher who has collected a long-form audio recording dataset and retains ownership and primary control over that dataset. Corpus collectors are the data originators and remain responsible for compliance with the ethical and legal terms under which their data was originally collected. |
| **Custodians** | Researchers granted access to raw audio recordings and human annotations from member datasets for the purpose of model development and benchmarking. In the current consortium, the custodians are Okko Räsänen, Marvin Lavechin, and Sho Tsuji. |
| **ExELang Consortium** | A research consortium through which corpus collectors grant custodians access to raw audio recordings and human annotations for the purpose of model development and benchmarking. |
| **Scientific Archives** | A recognized repository where corpus collectors deposit their datasets to ensure that all data access is legally and ethically governed. The ExELang Consortium requires that datasets be deposited in one of three archives compatible with long-form audio recordings: HomeBank, Databrary, or the Language Archive. |
| **ELSI** | A software platform providing a web interface and API for dataset management, model execution, and metric extraction. |
| **EL1000 Consortium** | A separate consortium governing the sharing of derived, deidentified metrics from language acquisition corpora (not raw audio). Membership in EL1000 is independent of membership in the ExELang Consortium. |
