# 4. Rights and Responsibilities of Each Party

## Corpus Collectors

### Rights

- Retain ownership and primary managerial control of their dataset at all times.
- Withdraw their dataset from the ExELang Consortium at any time by revoking custodian access through the archive.
- Be informed of and offered participation in (or the right to withdraw from) any proposed use of their data beyond model development and benchmarking.
- Receive formal citation and acknowledgement in all publications using their data.

### Responsibilities

- Ensure the dataset is deposited in a recognized scientific archive before or at the time of joining the consortium.
- Ensure that the archive's access conditions are consistent with the data-sharing arrangement described in this document.
- Serve as the primary point of contact for all third-party access requests to their dataset. Corpus collectors must not delegate this responsibility to the custodians (see Section 6).
- Maintain up-to-date contact information accessible to the custodians.


## Custodians

### Rights

- Access raw audio recordings and human annotations from member datasets for the purpose of model development and benchmarking.
- Publish results of model development and benchmarking research in academic venues (journals, conference proceedings) with appropriate citation of all data sources, without requiring corpus collectors as co-authors, provided the research falls within the defined scope (Section 2.4).
- Apply automated models to member datasets and retain derived annotations for research purposes.

### Responsibilities

- Open-source models developed using consortium data when feasible and consistent with applicable intellectual property policies.
- Notify corpus collectors and offer co-authorship or data withdrawal when proposed research extends beyond model development and benchmarking.
- Ensure proper citation and acknowledgement of all data sources in every publication.
- Comply with the legal and ethical access conditions of the scientific archives through which data access is granted.
- Decline all third-party requests for access to corpus collector data and direct such requests to the relevant corpus collector and/or archive (see Section 6).
